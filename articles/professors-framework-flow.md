# Professor's Strategic Framework Flow — Class Notes Diagram

## The Two Fundamental Questions
1. Who am I and what do I stand for?
2. Where am I going and why?

Nature of strategic decisions: affect the long-term.

## Time Horizons
- Short-term: Defend/Protect/Consolidate
- Medium-term: Developing, create options
- Long-term: Seeding

## The Strategic Process (Professor's exact flow)

### Phase 1 — External Analysis (Context/Environment)
- **PESTEL** — macro environment analysis
- **VUCA** — Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity
- **Porter+** — industry analysis (7 forces including Technology + Regulation)
- **C1-C4** — competition analysis levels

### Phase 2 — Internal Analysis (Strategic Position)
Three pillars feeding into Strategic Position:
1. **Capabilities** — Critical assets, Resources and distinctive capabilities, Privileged relationships
   - Test with **VRIN**: Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Not substitutable
   - External vs Internal resources
2. **Culture** — Cultural Web:
   1. Rituals and Routines
   2. Stories
   3. Symbols
   4. Power Structure
   5. Organizational Structure
   6. Control Systems
3. **Purpose** — Vision, Mission, Values

### Phase 3 — SWOT Analysis (SYNTHESIS)
SWOT bridges External Analysis + Internal Analysis:
- **Strengths** (from capabilities, culture, purpose)
- **Weaknesses** (from capabilities, culture, purpose)
- **Opportunities** (from PESTEL, VUCA, Porter+, C1-C4)
- **Threats** (from PESTEL, VUCA, Porter+, C1-C4)

SWOT is NOT an analysis tool. It is the SYNTHESIS of all prior analysis.

### Phase 4 — Strategic Objectives
Informed by SWOT analysis:
1. Leading vs Lagging indicators
2. Qualitative vs Quantitative metrics
3. Balanced Scorecard

### Phase 5 — 7S Tool (Implementation)
- Structure, Strategy, Style, Staff, Systems, Skills, Shared Values
- Apply PESTEL analysis to implementation context
- Political, Economic, Social, Legal factors affecting execution

### Phase 6 — Strategic Choices
The big circle of choices:
- **Business Strategies** — Generic strategies, Value Curves
- **Corporate Strategies** — BCG Matrix, Ansoff, Conglomerate diversification, Ashridge matrix
- **Internationalisation** — CAGE framework, Entrance ways/modes, Motivators/Inhibitors
- **Innovation** — S-Curve, Adoption Curve, Life Cycle, FCF Curve
- **Mergers & Acquisitions** — Strategic motivators, Inhibitors, Strategic alliance types (Access, Complementarity, Collusive, Scale)

## Key Strategic Tools Referenced

### BCG Matrix
- High growth + Cash: Stars
- Low growth + Cash: Cash Cows
- High growth + Small market share: Question Marks (Dogs)
- Big market share + Low growth: Cash Cows

### Ansoff Matrix
- Current market + Current products: Market Penetration
- Current market + New products: Development of Products
- New market + Current products: Market Development
- New market + New products: Diversification

### Ashridge Matrix (Parenting)
- Heartland, Ballast, Value-trap, Alien businesses

### Porter Generic Strategies / U-Curve
- Cost Leadership, Differentiation, Focus
- Deep Shit Valley = Stuck in the Middle

### Value Curves
- Low-cost re-differentiation — the queen's skill, resources, capabilities

### Ethics Framework
- Varies by: individual, culture/country/religion, time, business

### Aspirations Based Business Planning
- Critical points (pontos críticos)
- Key uncertainties
- Cross variables
- Name scenarios and describe them — "A Day in the Life"
- "Apostar" / "Bet" on scenarios
- Winners/Losers analysis
- Gaps analysis
- Tactical planning

### Internationalisation Flow
1. Strategic motivation
2. Motivators
3. Inhibitors
4. CAGE Framework (Cultural, Administrative/Political, Geographical, Economic distance)
5. Entrance ways/modes
→ Connect with Ansoff matrix
→ Apply CAGE analysis
→ Ask Why, Where, and How
→ Disruptive vs Incremental, Product vs Service, Open vs Close
→ Diffusion: gradually/incrementally (5 stages)

### M&A Framework
1. Strategic Motivators
2. Motivators
3. Inhibitors
4. Strategic Alliance types: Access, Complementarity, Collusive, Scale (inputs/outputs)

### Curves
- **S-Curve**: Tipping point analysis
- **FCF Curve**: Break-even and tipping points
- **Adoption Curve**: Innovators → Early Adopters → Early Majority → Large Majority → Decline
- **Life Cycle**: Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline (with rejuvenation possible)
- **Antigravity Curve**: Tipping point is around free
