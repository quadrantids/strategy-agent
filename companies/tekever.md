# Tekever -- Strategic Profile

## Company Overview -- European Defense Drone Unicorn, EUR 1.2B Valuation, 1,300 Employees

- **Full name**: Tekever Group (previously Tekever, Lda.)
- **Founded**: 2001
- **Founders**: Ricardo Mendes (CEO), Pedro Sinogas, Vitor Cristina -- alumni of Computer and Electrical Engineering at Instituto Superior Tecnico (IST), University of Lisbon
- **Headquarters**: Lisbon, Portugal (significant operations in UK)
- **Status**: **Unicorn** since May 2025 (7th Portuguese unicorn, 1st in DefTech)
- **Valuation**: >EUR 1.2B (USD 1.33B as of May 2025)
- **Employees**: ~1,300 (2026), up from ~1,000 in early 2025
- **Core business**: Medium Altitude Long Endurance (MALE) drones for security and defense
- **Position**: Largest European company in defense/security drones
- **Business model**: Drone-as-a-Service (DaaS) + equipment sales; 100% B2B/B2G

### Strategic Pivot History
- **2001-2009**: General technology company (AI, communication networks, distributed systems)
- **2009**: Pivoted to Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs/drones)
- **2019**: First operational deployment (English Channel surveillance for UK)
- **2022+**: Rapid scaling driven by Ukraine war demand and European defense spending increase

## Key Strategic Issues (for Exam)

1. **Scaling challenge**: Growing from startup to EUR 670M in committed investments across 4 countries is enormously complex. Can management scale fast enough while maintaining "weekly cycles" of innovation?
2. **Capital intensity vs. profitability**: EUR 670M in commitments with ~EUR 110M revenue. When does this become profitable? No disclosed path to profitability amid heavy CAPEX.
3. **Competition from defense primes**: Airbus Eurodrone, Leonardo Falco, Baykar -- can a Portuguese startup compete with EUR 50B+ conglomerates long-term?
4. **US market entry**: CEO says "Tekever cannot be a global player without significant US presence." How to enter the most competitive defense market in the world?
5. **Geopolitical dependency**: Revenue depends on European defense spending and Ukraine conflict. What if peace breaks out or budgets are cut?
6. **Talent war**: Competing for AI and aeronautical engineers with Google, Meta, Boeing, Airbus. Portuguese salaries (~EUR 35-45K) vs. UK/Germany (~EUR 60-80K).
7. **Ethical considerations**: Drones in combat zones raise moral questions. How to manage public perception and employee sentiment?
8. **DaaS vs. equipment sales**: DaaS (EMSA, UK Home Office) builds recurring revenue and lock-in, but big contracts (StormShroud, Ukraine) are traditional procurement. Which model should dominate?
9. **Customer concentration**: UK government likely >50% of revenue. Mitigating via EMSA, France, Spain, but risk remains.
10. **Independence vs. acquisition target**: NIF backing and 4-country footprint make Tekever strategically important, but at EUR 1.2B it's affordable for Airbus, Leonardo, or BAE Systems. Is independence sustainable?
