# Grupo NORS -- Strategic Profile

## Company Overview -- Family-Owned Volvo Distributor, 91-Year Partnership, 17 Countries

- **Full name**: Nors Group, S.A. (formerly Auto Sueco, renamed to Nors in 2013 at holding level)
- **Founded**: 1933 (informal founding); formal registration of Auto Sueco in 1949
- **Founder**: Luiz Oscar Jervell (Norwegian born in Porto, Portuguese mother from Boticas)
- **Headquarters**: Porto, Portugal
- **CEO**: Tomas Jervell (grandson of founder, CEO since 2009)
- **Controlling shareholders**: Jervell and Jensen families (private company)
- **Employees**: 3,152 (consolidated) / 4,200+ (aggregate incl. Ascendum JV)
- **Presence**: 17 countries, 4 continents (Europe, Americas, Africa, Turkey)
- **Volvo partnership**: 91+ years as Volvo's first private importer globally

## Recent Strategic Moves (2024-2026) -- Brand Unification, Canadian Dominance, Segment Reorganization

### 1. Brand Consolidation (October 2024)
- **Eliminated 17 commercial brands** in favor of single global brand "NORS"
- Retired brands: Auto Sueco Portugal, Galius, Civiparts, AS Parts, Onedrive, Nors Centro Oeste, Agrofito, Agronew, Auto Maquinaria, Auto Sueco Sao Paulo, Kinlai, Auto Sueco Angola, Auto Sueco Botswana, Auto Sueco Mocambique, Auto Sueco Namibia, GreatWest, StrongCo
- Objective: create unified global identity across all 17 countries
- Risk: potential loss of local brand recognition and customer relationships built over decades
- Unprecedented in the distribution industry -- most global dealers maintain local identities (Finning kept its name in UK as "Finning UK")

### 2. Reorganization into 5 Segments
- Shifted from geography-based to business-type-based structure
- Created **Nors Ventures** for exploring opportunities beyond core
- Clear separation between new equipment sales and aftermarket services
- Enables global benchmarking and best practice transfer across geographies

### 3. Canadian Expansion (2020-2025)

**Result**: 37 branches and 750+ employees in Canada, covering 80%+ of Canadian market (west to east). North American market (USA + Canada) now represents ~28% of group revenue.

### 4. Record Results with Renault Trucks (2024)
- Achieved historic best results as Renault Trucks distributor
- Growth particularly strong in Spain and Portugal
- Strengthens multi-OEM strategy beyond exclusive Volvo dependency

### 5. 2030 Strategy Pillars

## Key Strategic Issues (for exam)

1. **Brand consolidation risk**: Eliminating 17 established local brands for one global "Nors" brand -- will customers in Angola, Brazil, and Canada identify with a Portuguese brand? Was this the right trade-off between global efficiency and local identity?
2. **OEM dependency**: Fundamental dependency on Volvo Group contracts. If Volvo changes distribution strategy (direct sales, digital platforms), Nors's model is threatened. How to mitigate? Volvo's 2025 strategy continues to emphasize dealer partnerships.
3. **Cyclicality management**: 64% in construction equipment is highly cyclical. How to smooth earnings through the cycle? (Aftermarket is the answer, but execution matters)
4. **Electrification transition**: Need to retool for servicing electric trucks and equipment. Nors must invest in new capabilities while the old combustion fleet still dominates.
5. **Africa risk**: Historical presence in Angola/Mozambique becoming liability vs. asset due to political instability and currency crises.
6. **Nors Ventures**: What should the exploratory business unit focus on? Energy? Mobility? How much to invest in non-core opportunities?
7. **Net income decline**: Dropped from EUR 110M to EUR 68.6M. Is this cyclical or structural? How to restore profitability while investing in growth?
8. **Aftermarket margin gap**: Nors at ~30-35% aftermarket revenue mix vs. Finning at 54% and Toromont at ~50%. Closing this gap could add EUR 50-80M EBITDA.

## Framework-Ready Data -- VRIN, BCG, Porter's Five Forces, Ansoff, Generic Strategy

### VRIN Analysis
- **V (Valuable)**: 91-year Volvo partnership; global distribution network in 17 countries; aftermarket know-how
- **R (Rare)**: World's first private Volvo importer; simultaneous presence across diverse markets (Brazil, Angola, Canada)
- **I (Inimitable)**: 9-decade institutional relationships with Volvo impossible to replicate; reputation built over generations
- **N (Non-substitutable)**: Exclusive distribution contracts; service infrastructure in hard-to-access markets (Africa)

### BCG Matrix
- **Star**: Construction Equipment in USA/Canada (high growth, strong position via acquisitions)
- **Cash Cow**: Trucks & Buses in Portugal (mature market, market leader)
- **Question Mark**: Agro (high potential in Brazil, 6.8% market share, limited current position)
- **Question Mark**: Nors Ventures (exploratory)
- **Dog candidate**: Some African operations (declining economies, political risk)

### Porter's Five Forces (Distribution Industry)
- **Rivalry**: Medium (limited number of Volvo distributors per geography; but compete with Cat/Deere dealers)
- **Supplier Power**: Very High (Volvo/Renault Trucks are essential -- no alternative OEMs to represent)
- **Buyer Power**: Medium (large fleet buyers have negotiating power; but equipment is specialized)
- **Threat of Substitutes**: Low-Medium (alternative equipment brands, but switching costs are high)
- **Barriers to Entry**: High (exclusive distribution agreements, service network investment, OEM relationships)

### Ansoff Matrix
- **Market Penetration**: Grow aftermarket share in existing markets; increase Renault Trucks sales
- **Market Development**: Further Canadian acquisitions; potentially Australia, Middle East (Saudi Arabia awarded USD 55B in projects in 2024, +57% YoY)
- **Product Development**: Electric vehicle servicing; telematics/predictive maintenance; digital services
- **Diversification**: Nors Ventures exploring adjacencies (energy, urban mobility)

### Generic Strategy (Porter)
**Focus differentiation**: Nors differentiates within the Volvo distribution ecosystem through service quality, geographic reach, and aftermarket excellence. Not competing on cost but on relationship depth and coverage breadth.

## Supply Chain & Operational Model

### How the Distribution Model Works
Nors operates as an **authorized distributor** within the Volvo Group ecosystem:
1. **Equipment sourcing**: New equipment procured directly from Volvo Group, Renault Trucks, Sennebogen -- no manufacturing capability
2. **Parts & aftermarket**: OEM parts sourced from Volvo's global parts distribution network; some third-party parts
3. **Service delivery**: Workshop and field service operations across 37+ branches (Canada alone), with trained technicians
4. **Rental fleet**: Owned rental fleet of equipment, creating asset-heavy balance sheet exposure

### Multi-Brand Equipment Portfolio (Canada)

| Brand | Equipment Category |
|-------|-------------------|
| Volvo CE | Excavators, wheel loaders, haulers, pavers (primary OEM) |
| Case Construction | Backhoes, loaders, excavators |
| Sennebogen | Material handlers, cranes |
| Manitowoc (National, Grove) | Mobile cranes (60+ year distribution relationship) |
| Fassi | Truck-mounted cranes |
| Konecranes | Forklifts, lift trucks |
| Eco Log | Forestry equipment |
| Rammer | Hydraulic hammers |

### Operational Efficiency Programs
- **Flow Program**: Cultural transformation for after-sales/workshop operations; deployed in Portugal and Angola; focuses on workshop productivity, service truck utilization, parts availability
- **CORE Digital Transformation (since 2017)**: 4 implementation waves covering ERP modernization, telematics integration, digital customer interfaces, data analytics
- **Supplier Portal**: Operational in Portugal, Angola, and Southern Africa
- **NORS SUN**: Three-stage methodology (Suspend, Understand, Nurture) for identifying disruptive business model opportunities
