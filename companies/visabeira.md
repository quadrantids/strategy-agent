## Company Overview -- EUR 2.4B Conglomerate, 18 Countries, Founded 1980 in Viseu

- **Full name**: Grupo Visabeira, S.A.
- **Founded**: May 12, 1980 by Fernando Campos Nunes and brother Daniel in Viseu
- **Headquarters**: Viseu, Portugal
- **Founder/Chairman**: Fernando Campos Nunes (7th richest person in Portugal 2025, fortune ~EUR 1.898B per Forbes; 18th Most Powerful Person in Portugal per Jornal de Negocios)

- **Presence**: 18 countries, 5 continents
- **Structure**: Diversified conglomerate with 3 sub-holdings
- **Private**: Not publicly listed at group level (Vista Alegre is listed separately on Euronext Lisbon)
- **Credit rating**: BBB- stable (ARC Ratings, Sept 2024); A-3 short-term

## Key Strategic Issues (for exam)

1. **Conglomerate discount vs. synergy**: Does combining engineering, ceramics, tourism, and real estate create or destroy value? Goldman Sachs invested only in Nearing -- implying the market values it separately. SOTP analysis suggests group value of EUR 2,400-2,500M with limited conglomerate discount since Nearing = 85-90% of total value.
2. **Organic vs. acquisitive growth**: 18% organic growth at EUR 2.2B is exceptional -- but with EUR 5.2B backlog and Goldman Sachs capital, should they pursue larger M&A?
3. **Founder dependency**: Fernando Campos Nunes (69) is central; has never given a public interview. Group-level succession unclear beyond son at Vista Alegre.
4. **Geographic concentration risk**: USA + UK = 55% of core revenue. Brexit, US political cycles, and FX exposure (USD, GBP on 55% of revenue; 10% EUR appreciation = ~EUR 120M revenue reduction) are material risks.
5. **Rebranding execution**: Constructel to Nearing -- will customers and employees embrace the new identity across 11 countries?
6. **Energy vs. Telecom balance**: Energy at 45% and growing faster -- should the group pivot more aggressively toward energy transition?
7. **EBITDA margin improvement**: 11% is below Quanta (~12%) and well below industry leaders (~15-18%). Can AI and scale drive expansion?
8. **IPO timing**: Goldman Sachs typical holding period is 5-7 years (entry 2022); IPO window likely 2027-2029. EUR 2B+ valuation could reach EUR 3-5B at listed multiples.
9. **US integration risk**: Sargent Electric alone added 1,000+ employees and $400M+ revenue -- execution critical.
