# DIGI Communications -- Strategic Profile

## Company Overview -- Romanian Telecom Disruptor, Build-Own-Operate Model, 7 European Markets

- **Full name**: Digi Communications N.V.
- **Founded**: 1992 in Romania (as TVS Holding Brasov -- cable television)
- **Founder & controlling shareholder**: Zoltan Teszari (Romanian-Hungarian entrepreneur)
- **Headquarters**: Bucharest, Romania (N.V. registered in Netherlands)
- **Listed**: Bucharest Stock Exchange (ticker: DIGI.RO)
- **Market capitalization**: ~EUR 3B (March 2026)
- **Employees**: ~24,448 (2025, group-wide)
- **Portugal commercial launch**: November 4, 2024
- **Business model**: Build-own-operate telecom networks; disruptive low-cost pricing
- **Total RGUs**: 32 million (end 2025)

### Core Differentiating Model
DIGI builds and operates its **own fiber-to-the-home (FTTH) networks end-to-end**, unlike MVNOs that lease infrastructure. This enables:
- Significantly lower operating costs
- Consumer prices 3-5x below incumbents
- Operational EBITDA margins of ~26-30% at group level despite low pricing
- Over 90% fiber penetration in Romania
- Quality comparable to or exceeding incumbents

## Key Strategic Issues (for exam)

1. **Christensen disruption in action**: DIGI is a textbook case of low-end disruption. Entered with "good enough" service at 3-5x lower prices. Will it follow the Iliad trajectory and capture 15-20% market share?
2. **Path to profitability in Portugal**: Costs 68% above revenue, EUR 7 ARPU, -67% EBITDA margin. How long until break-even? Is the model sustainable at these prices?
3. **Build vs. buy infrastructure**: DIGI couldn't get a roaming deal and had to buy Nowo (EUR 150M) and build from scratch. Was this forced strategy ultimately advantageous (full network control)?
4. **Incumbent response**: MEO/NOS/Vodafone launched sub-brands (Uzo/WOO/Amigo) with matching prices. Can DIGI maintain price leadership as incumbents respond?
5. **Content gap**: Incumbents control Sport TV and premium content. How can DIGI compete for customers who value sports/content?
6. **Multi-market expansion risk**: Simultaneous entry in Portugal, Italy, Belgium, UK while Spain still scaling. Is DIGI spreading too thin?
7. **Governance risk**: Concentrated ownership (65% Teszari), acquitted CEO corruption case -- do these create investor/regulatory risk?
8. **ARPU trajectory**: EUR 7/month is 5-7x below incumbents. Can DIGI increase ARPU while maintaining "low-cost" positioning? ARPU has been declining (EUR 7.60 at launch to EUR 7.10 in H1 2025) as more low-tier mobile-only users added.

## Framework-Ready Data -- VRIN, BCG, Porter's Five Forces, Christensen, Generic Strategy

### VRIN Analysis
- **V (Valuable)**: Own FTTH network; ultra-efficient cost structure; proven multi-market disruption playbook
- **R (Rare)**: Ability to offer 3-5x lower prices while maintaining quality; 30-year track record of disruption
- **I (Inimitable)**: Culture of extreme cost efficiency is organizational DNA; building networks from scratch requires massive capital and expertise
- **N (Non-substitutable)**: Fiber network is physical infrastructure (once built, lasting asset); spectrum licenses are limited resource

### Disruptive Innovation (Christensen)
DIGI is a **classic low-end disruptor**:
1. Enters at the bottom of the market (price-sensitive customers, immigrants, young people)
2. Offers "good enough" service at dramatically lower prices
3. Gradually improves service (adds content, speeds, coverage)
4. Incumbents initially dismiss or respond with sub-brands rather than fundamental change
5. Eventually captures significant market share as mainstream customers switch
6. Incumbents face "innovator's dilemma" -- matching prices would destroy their margins

### BCG Matrix (by Market)
- **Cash Cow**: Romania (dominant, mature, EUR 556M EBITDA, ~46% margin)
- **Star**: Spain (EUR 929M revenue, growing 24% YoY, approaching profitability)
- **Question Mark**: Portugal (850K RGUs, losing money, high potential)
- **Question Mark**: Italy/Belgium/UK (very early stage)

### Porter's Five Forces (Portuguese Telecom)
- **Rivalry**: Very High (4 operators in small market; DIGI forcing price competition)
- **Supplier Power**: Medium (Huawei, Nokia, Ericsson for network equipment)
- **Buyer Power**: High (consumers can switch easily; DIGI reduced switching costs to 3 months)
- **Threat of Substitutes**: Low (telecom is essential service)
- **Barriers to Entry**: Very High (spectrum licenses, network investment ~EUR 500M+; but DIGI already overcame them)

### Generic Strategy (Porter)
**Cost leadership**: DIGI competes primarily on cost. Own-network model enables structural cost advantage. This is not "low prices funded by losses" -- it's a fundamentally more efficient operating model proven profitable in Romania (46% EBITDA margin).

## Supply Chain & Operations -- Vertically Integrated Network Builder

### Network Infrastructure Model
DIGI operates a **vertically integrated, build-own-operate** telecom model:

1. **FTTH (Fiber-to-the-Home)**: Builds own fiber optic network end-to-end
   - Romania: 90%+ fiber penetration
   - Spain: 13.7M homes passed (2025); targeting 21M by 2030
   - Portugal: Own FTTH under construction; leveraging Nowo's legacy infrastructure
   - Belgium: Partnered with Citymesh for network buildout

2. **5G Mobile Network**: Own infrastructure
   - Portugal: 2,385+ stations, ~40% population coverage
   - Romania: Operational since 2019
   - Spain: Transitioned from MVNO to MNO in 2025 via spectrum + Telefonica RAN-sharing deal

3. **Equipment suppliers**: Nokia, Ericsson, Huawei

### Capex Investment Profile

| Year | Capex | Capex/Revenue | Key Investments |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2024 | EUR 885M | ~46% | Peak year; Portugal launch, Spain FTTH, Belgium entry |
| 2025 | EUR 798M | ~36% | Continued buildout; declining from peak |
| 2026 (target) | ~EUR 400M (recurring) | ~16% | Capex intensity normalizing |
| 2028 (target) | ~EUR 250M (Spain alone) | <10% | Near steady-state |

### Retail Presence (Portugal)
- Only 2 official stores as of end 2025 -- extremely lean physical retail
- Plans to open more stores in 2026
- Primarily digital/online acquisition model
