# Vista Alegre Atlantis (VAA) -- Strategic Profile

## Company Overview -- Founded 1824, Oldest Iberian Porcelain Maker, 83% Visabeira + 10% CR7

- **Full name**: VAA - Vista Alegre Atlantis, SGPS, S.A.
- **Founded**: 1824 by Jose Ferreira Pinto Basto (oldest porcelain factory in the Iberian Peninsula)
- **Headquarters**: Ilhavo, Aveiro district, Portugal
- **Listed**: Euronext Lisbon (ticker: VAF)
- **Parent group**: Grupo Visabeira (holding controlled by Fernando Campos Nunes, 83.07% stake)
- **Notable minority shareholder**: Cristiano Ronaldo (10% via CR7 SA, acquired June 2024 for EUR 17.3M)
- **Factories**: 6 production units in Portugal (all in Ilhavo region)
- **Annual production**: ~10 million pieces
- **Employees**: 2,304 (H1 2025, declining from 2,373 in H1 2024)
- **Structure**: 16 companies organized in 4 business areas

### Business Areas and Brands

| Business Area | Key Brand | 2024 Revenue Share | 9M 2025 Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stoneware (Gres) | Vista Alegre | 43.5% (~EUR 59.5M) | +15.1% |

### History Milestones

- 1824: Factory founded at Quinta da Ermida, Ilhavo
- 2001: Merger with Atlantis (crystal) group, forming the largest Iberian tableware group and 6th largest globally
- 2009: Grupo Visabeira acquires controlling stake
- 2024: CR7 acquires 10% stake; board expanded from 14 to 15 members to include CR7 representative
- 2026 (Feb): CEO transition -- Fernando Daniel Campos Nunes (founder's son) replaces Nuno Terras Marques

## Competitive Landscape -- VAA vs. Villeroy & Boch, Fiskars Vita, Bernardaud, Meissen

### Global Tableware Industry

- Global luxury tableware market: USD 25.0B (2025), CAGR 5.3%, projected USD 39.9B (2034)
- Porcelain tableware market: USD 10.8B (2025), CAGR 7.2%
- Ceramic tableware (broad): USD 18.8B (2025), CAGR 5.6%
- Europe holds 32.2% of porcelain market (largest region); Asia-Pacific 28% (fastest growing)
- Energy-intensive industry (ceramic kilns dependent on natural gas) creating cost pressures
- Vista Alegre positions itself as one of the 5 largest and most profitable tableware manufacturers globally

### Key Competitive Developments

- **Fiskars Vita separation**: Operational separation effective April 1, 2025, legal completion by end Q1 2026. If IPO proceeds, creates a EUR 600M+ pure-play competitor with 4x VAA's scale. Expected annual cost savings: EUR 12M.
- **Bernardaud-Haviland consolidation** (Dec 2024): Merger of two top Limoges porcelain houses strengthens French competition in ultra-luxury.
- **V&B negative guidance**: Expects 2026 revenue decline "mid to high single digits" and guided EBIT to EUR 75-85M (vs. EUR 97.8M in 2025) -- canary in the coal mine for the entire industry.

### Vista Alegre's Competitive Position

- **Highest gross margin in industry** (67.2%) -- reflects Portuguese cost base (~60% of German labor cost) and premium brand pricing
- **Only company combining 4 materials** (porcelain, faience, stoneware, crystal) under one group
- **CR7 partnership is unique** -- no competitor has comparable celebrity alignment
- **Smallest scale** among major global players -- vulnerability to cost shocks
- **200-year brand heritage** is a genuine differentiator in the luxury segment
- **EBITDA margin ~20%** is highest in industry vs. V&B Dining at ~10% and Fiskars Vita at ~4.5%

|---|---|---|
| PRR-funded phase | EUR 14.8M total | EUR 9.9M executed (67%) |

**Net investment after grants**: EUR 14.8M - EUR 7.1M = EUR 7.7M net capex. If annual energy savings reach EUR 1.5-2.0M, payback is 4-5 years on net basis.

| Scenario | Assumption | Payback Period |
|---|---|---|
| Optimistic | Energy savings 20%, gas prices high | ~5 years (net positive from year 3 with grants) |
| Base case | Energy savings 15%, gas prices moderate | ~7 years |
| Pessimistic | Savings 10%, gas prices fall | ~10+ years; questionable ROI |

### Sustainability-Linked Bond

- Issued May 2024: retail green bond, EUR 60M, 5-year maturity, 5.30% gross fixed rate
- KPI: Tons of Oil Equivalent per Ton of Good Parts (target Dec 2028)
- Penalty if missed: additional EUR 1.25 per bond on repayment date
- Creates a "double or nothing" dynamic -- succeed and margins improve; fail and both the penalty and continued high energy costs compound

### Industry Technology Frontiers

- Hydrogen-fired kilns: reduce emissions ~30% but add ~EUR 115/ton; still experimental
- Electric kilns: currently "economically unsound" due to electricity vs. gas cost differential in EU
- Hybrid heat-pump kilns: up to 65% energy savings; most promising near-term technology
- Microwave-assisted drying: up to 99% energy reduction in drying phase; promising for supplementary use

### Energy & Industry Context

- Energy = ~30% of ceramic production costs (industry average)
- Portuguese ceramic industry = ~1,000 companies, 4% of national natural gas consumption, 17% of industrial gas consumption
- Portugal is the #1 European manufacturer of ceramic tableware

## Key Strategic Issues (for exam)

1. **"Profitless growth" paradox**: Revenue up +9.4% to EUR 152M but EBITDA declining to ~EUR 25M and net income stuck at EUR 3.8M. The market is paying 49x trailing earnings for a company with declining margins. How to protect profitability?
2. **CR7 partnership execution**: 22 months after announcement, no revenue from Middle East/Asia JV disclosed. Unprecedented celebrity partnership for tableware -- will it drive sales or is it brand washing?
3. **Crystal & Glass decline** (-12.3%): Structural decline as formal entertaining wanes globally -- divest or restructure?
4. **Geographic concentration of production**: All 6 factories in Ilhavo region creates single-point-of-failure risk
5. **Energy transition cost**: EUR 50M decarbonization is ~37% of annual revenue; EUR 60M SLB bond costs EUR 3.18M/year (nearly equal to net income). Net Debt/EBITDA deteriorated to 3.6x.
6. **Ownership concentration**: 93% locked (83% Visabeira + 10% CR7); governance risks; ~7% free float suppresses valuation
7. **Stoneware as growth engine vs. IKEA dependency**: 43.5% of revenue and growing fastest, but most IKEA-dependent segment
8. **Fiskars Vita separation**: Creates a EUR 600M+ pure-play competitor with 4x VAA's scale
9. **V&B negative guidance**: Industry headwinds signaled by largest competitor expecting "mid to high single digit" revenue decline in 2026

### Raw Materials & Energy

- **Ceramic raw materials**: Clays, feldspars, kaolins -- sourced domestically and from Europe
- **Natural gas**: Primary fuel for ceramic kilns (~2/3 of energy consumption is thermal)
- **Electricity**: ~1/3 of energy consumption; transitioning to solar
- **Energy cost impact**: +26-27% YoY increase -- primary margin compression driver

### Concentration Risk

- **Geographic**: 6/6 factories in Ilhavo area (100% production concentration)
- **Customer**: IKEA = single largest B2B client (~25-30% of total revenue estimated)
- **Energy**: Natural gas = ~2/3 of thermal energy (100% fossil for kilns)
- **Personnel**: 2,304 employees, overwhelmingly in Ilhavo region
- **Ownership**: 93% locked (Visabeira 83% + CR7 10%)
